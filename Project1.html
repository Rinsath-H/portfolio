Iot Based Industrial Pollution Monitoring and control System

Introductions

The rapid pace of industrialization has undeniably contributed to economic growth, employment generation, and technological advancement. However, it has also brought significant environmental consequences. One of the major concerns is industrial pollution, which includes emissions of harmful gases, particulate matter, and discharge of toxic substances into water bodies and soil. These pollutants pose a severe risk to human health, biodiversity, and overall ecosystem balance.

Traditionally, pollution monitoring has relied on manual data collection and laboratory analysis, which are often time-consuming, infrequent, and prone to errors. With the advent of the Internet of Things (IoT), there is an opportunity to revolutionize environmental monitoring by automating data collection, enabling real-time analysis, and facilitating timely decision-making.

An IoT-based Industrial Pollution Monitoring System uses embedded sensors, wireless communication, and cloud computing to monitor and report pollution levels in real-time. These systems can detect gases such as CO₂, CO, SO₂, NOx, and particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10), and transmit data to a central server or cloud platform for analysis and visualization. The integration of such systems can help industries comply with environmental regulations, avoid penalties, and contribute to sustainable development.
